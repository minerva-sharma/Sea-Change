Animal Rights Education

Most updated articles about animal research and animal rights

Books

Give a Sh*t: Do Good. Live Better. Save the Planet. by Ashlee Piper: on a local level, the book provides tune-ups for a more sustainable, compassionate, and ethical life, with tips regarding home and outside personal practices

Life Behind Bars Vol. 1 by Julian Starks: contains 100 images of animals in captivity and their realities, providing a realistic glimpse into how life in captivity plays out and saves certain species

Making a Killing: The Political Economy of Animal Rights by Bob Torres: provides insight into the intersections between human and animal oppressions in relation to the exploitative dynamics of capitalism.

Movies

Blackfish and Long Gone Wild: Blackfish discusses the plight of captive orcas at SeaWorld, while Long Gone Wild takes a look at the effects of the film, centering on five primary areas: (1) The Blackfish Effect (what it did and didn't accomplish i.e., SeaWorld took a major hit to its bottom line, but the 20 orcas are still there); (2) The case against captivity (orcas are forced to live in barren concrete tanks); (3) Orcas as sentient animals (their great capacity to think, feel, communicate, and empathize); (4) The Whale Sanctuary Project and its model seaside sanctuary for retired orcas â€“ providing a safe, permanent home in their natural habitat; and, (5) The ominous threat to orcas posed by Russia and China, triggered by the explosive growth of mega-aquariums in China. (longgonewild.com)

Off the Chain: Imparts perspectives of both those who perpetuate dogfighting and those against the subculture and sport of dogfighting for its abuse of pit bulls.

Eating Animals: investigates factory farming and its harmful impacts on both the environment around factories and animals bread for consumption--spoiler alert: yes, companies can get away with spraying animal waste pollutants into the atmosphere.

(More info at The Washington Post)

Podcasts

AR (Animal Rights) Zone features workshops over individual animal rights issues

The Bearded Vegans Humorous hosts Andy and Paul chat about food, news, and products before delving into one question or topic related to ethical veganism or treatment of animals

Knowing Animals Animal studies experts discuss their work and how the public can make a difference in the field of animal rights (from Striking at the Roots)
